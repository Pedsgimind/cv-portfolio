Professional Summary & Career Narrative

I am a board-certified pediatrician and pediatric gastroenterologist by the Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Canada (RCPSC), with a background in inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), eosinophilic gastrointestinal disorders, pediatric motility and functional testing, and complex nutrition, including enteral and parenteral support. Over the course of my career, I have managed a broad spectrum of gastrointestinal and hepatobiliary disorders in infants, children, and adolescents, while maintaining active involvement in general and emergency pediatrics in tertiary, regional, and military healthcare settings.

My subspecialty practice has been anchored in comprehensive, evidence-based pediatric gastroenterology. This includes advanced diagnostic and interventional endoscopy (upper and lower), PEG and PEC insertion, management of acute and chronic gastrointestinal bleeding, complex constipation and fecal incontinence, celiac disease, very early onset IBD, intestinal lymphangiectasia, and post–liver transplant and pancreatitis care. I also have experience with capsule endoscopy, pH–impedance monitoring, hydrogen/methane breath testing, and anorectal manometry, allowing me to integrate motility and functional testing into day-to-day clinical decision-making.

A major focus of my work has been the interface between gastrointestinal disease, nutrition, and neurodevelopment. I have developed particular interest and experience in feeding problems, selective and restrictive eating, obesity and metabolic risk, and gastrointestinal manifestations of Autism Spectrum Disorder and other neurodevelopmental conditions. I aim to deliver care that is not only technically and diagnostically sound, but also family-centered and trauma-informed, recognizing the impact of chronic GI symptoms on growth, development, schooling, and quality of life.

Teaching and systems-level work are important to my professional identity. I have been actively involved in designing and delivering structured education for pediatric residents and fellows, supervising case forums and journal clubs, leading board-oriented teaching sessions, and developing innovative formats such as podcast-based case learning. I have also led and supervised quality-improvement and research projects.

I bring a strong and well-rounded background in pediatrics and pediatric gastroenterology, with board certification in Pediatrics by the Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Canada and Licentiate of the Medical Council of Canada (LMCC) certification. My training and practice experiences across Canada, the Gulf region, and Iraq, together with earlier work in military and academic environments in Texas, have shaped a practice style that is collaborative, evidence-based, and highly responsive to the needs of children with complex gastrointestinal and nutritional disorders and their families.

Interventional Upper Endoscopy (EGD) Procedures
  • Esophageal dilation: treatment of esophageal strictures due to various etiologies, including esophageal atresia, achalasia, and gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)–related strictures.
  • Foreign body removal: removal of ingested foreign bodies lodged in the esophagus, stomach, or duodenum.
  • Percutaneous endoscopic gastrostomy (PEG) tube placement for long-term enteral nutrition.
  • Implemented an endoscopic PEG program at Maternity and Children Hospital in Burayda, Saudi Arabia, transitioning away from open surgical gastrostomy to a less invasive approach that reduces complications and is completed in approximately 15 minutes.
  • Management of esophageal varices: band ligation or sclerotherapy for the treatment of esophageal varices, often related to liver disease.
  • Treatment of bleeding lesions: hemostasis of bleeding ulcers and other lesions using injection, clipping, or cautery.
  • Biopsy: tissue sampling from the esophagus, stomach, or duodenum for diagnosing conditions such as celiac disease, eosinophilic esophagitis, or Helicobacter pylori.
  • Polypectomy: removal of polyps in the upper gastrointestinal tract.
Interventional Colonoscopy Procedures
  • Polypectomy: removal of polyps in the colon, including in conditions such as familial adenomatous polyposis and juvenile polyposis syndrome.
  • Biopsy: tissue sampling from the colon or terminal ileum for diagnosing inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), celiac disease, infections, and other pathology.
  • Stricture dilation: dilation of colonic strictures, including those secondary to Crohn’s disease.
  • Management of lower GI bleeding: hemostasis for bleeding lesions in the colon, using clipping, cautery, or injection therapy.
  • Foreign body removal: retrieval of foreign objects from the colon or rectum.
  • Percutaneous endoscopic cecostomy (PEC): placement of a cecostomy tube for the management of severe constipation and fecal incontinence, enabling antegrade colonic enemas to be administered directly into the colon.
